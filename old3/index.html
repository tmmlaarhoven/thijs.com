Home

Hi, and welcome to my website! I'm Thijs Laarhoven, a fourth-year Ph.D. student at the Eindhoven University of Technology, studying the unrelated topics of fingerprinting and group testing algorithms, lattice algorithms, and the Collatz conjecture.

Previously, I obtained the degrees of Bachelor of Science (2009) and Master of Science (2011) from the Eindhoven University of Technology, graduating with honors in Industrial and Applied Mathematics. On this website you can find stuff related to my research, such as my publications and slides of previous talks. The menu above may help you to find what you are looking for.

News

(December 2014) Continuing on the lattices and LSH line of research, we (Benne de Weger and myself) looked at different LSH algorithms than the angular LSH method, to see if other algorithms lead to a further speed-up. Two (recent) methods were found to offer a significant asymptotic speed-up, leading to the best SVP time complexity to date.


(November 2014) We recently gave our PQCrypto 2013-paper about applying quantum search to SVP algorithms in lattices a major overhaul, where we included various recent results from the area of sieving. The resulting paper, titled "Finding shortest lattice vectors faster using quantum search" can be found under Publications.


(September 2014) Back to lattices! There is a new paper in the Publications-section about applying angular locality-sensitive hashing (LSH) to sieving algorithms on lattices. In particular, using angular LSH we obtain a new record for solving SVP in high dimensions, with a (heuristic) time complexity of less than 2^(0.3366n).


(June 2014) The paper "Capacities and Capacity-Achieving Decoders for Various Fingerprinting Games" won the Best Student Paper Award at IH&MMSec 2014! The paper and slides of the presentation can be found under "Publications" and "Events" above.


(April 2014) The final versions of the results about capacities and decoders for fingerprinting and group testing are finally written up, and they can be found on the arXiv or under "Publications" above.


(January 2014) Over the last month some interesting results came up, such as explicit asymptotics for the fingerprinting capacities for some common attacks, and decoders which provably achieve these bounds. This lead to the newest publication "Capacities and Capacity-Achieving Decoders for Various Fingerprinting Games."


(November 2013) I recently went to the IEEE Workshop on Information Forensics and Security in Guangzhou, China, where I presented a paper as well. The slides and the paper are now available under "Publications" above.


(October 2013) In early October I went to Illinois to attend the 51st Allerton Conference on Communication, Control and Computing (in Monticello, IL) and briefly visited the UIUC in Urbana-Champaign to give a talk there. I've put slides of both presentations, as well as the final version of the Allerton paper online under "Publications."
