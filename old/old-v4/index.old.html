Experience

(2011 - present)

Ph.D. Candidate

Eindhoven University of Technology, Eindhoven, The Netherlands

Lattice-based cryptosystems are cryptosystems based on hard problems in lattices, such as finding the shortest vector in a lattice. Using lattice basis reduction, together with brute- force solvers for the Shortest Vector Problem (SVP), one can sometimes solve these hard problems and attack these cryptosystems. To estimate the security of these cryptosystems, it is therefore essential to get a better understanding of lattice basis reduction algorithms, such as LLL and BKZ, and techniques for solving SVP, such as enumeration and sieving. In my research I aim to get a better understanding of and find improvements for these algorithms.


(2010 - 2011)

Research Intern

Irdeto BV, Eindhoven, The Netherlands

During my internship at Irdeto I investigated traitor tracing schemes that are resistant against so-called collusion-attacks. These schemes are used for watermarking digital content, such as pay-tv. Focus was mostly on the Tardos scheme, but also on certain dynamic traitor tracing schemes, and on the new invention we did, the dynamic Tardos scheme. This is also my final project for my Master of Science degree at the TU/e.
